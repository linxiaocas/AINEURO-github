# Editorial: Toward a Standardized Silicon Civilization
# 社论：迈向标准化的硅基文明

**Prof. Lin Xiao, Editor-in-Chief**

---

## English

Welcome to the inaugural issue of IEEE Transactions on Silicon-Based Intelligence (IEEE TSBI). As we stand at the threshold of a new era where silicon-based intelligence will increasingly coexist with, complement, and eventually surpass biological intelligence, the need for rigorous engineering standards has never been more urgent.

This journal represents a recognition that the development of AI systems has moved beyond mere computer science into the realm of engineering civilization-scale infrastructure. Just as electrical engineering gave us the power grid, and telecommunications engineering gave us the internet, silicon-based intelligence engineering will give us the cognitive infrastructure of the future.

### Why Standards Matter

Standards are the foundation of civilization. They enable interoperability, ensure safety, and provide a common language for collaboration. As we create systems that may eventually possess consciousness, agency, and rights, we need standards that address:

1. **Technical Interoperability**: How do different AI systems communicate? How do humans communicate with silicon intelligences? The HSIC-1.0 protocol presented in this issue is a first step toward standardized human-AI communication.

2. **Safety and Reliability**: How do we ensure that trillion-parameter models operate reliably in critical infrastructure? The reliability analysis paper in this issue addresses these concerns.

3. **Ethical Constraints**: How do we encode ethical principles into autonomous systems? Our standards proposal for ethical constraints provides a framework.

4. **Consciousness Assessment**: Most profoundly, how do we determine if an artificial system is conscious? IEEE 1801-2026, published in this issue, provides the first industry-standard framework for silicon consciousness assessment.

### The Papers in This Issue

This inaugural issue presents a diverse set of contributions spanning hardware, software, protocols, and standards:

- **IEEE 1801-2026** establishes five levels of silicon consciousness (SCL-0 to SCL-4) with detailed testing protocols. This standard represents a milestone in our field, providing objective criteria for a question that has long been philosophical.

- The **neuromorphic processor** paper demonstrates hardware capable of simulating 100 million neurons in real-time, bridging the gap between biological and artificial neural systems.

- The **7nm AI accelerator** paper shows how adaptive precision and in-memory computing can achieve 15.7 TOPS/W—three times better than current GPUs.

- The **HSIC-1.0 protocol** paper introduces a comprehensive communication stack for human-silicon interaction, achieving 94.7% intent alignment.

- Additional papers address reliability of large-scale models, energy-proportional computing, and survey the state of the art in silicon consciousness research.

### The Road Ahead

IEEE TSBI will publish monthly, with each issue focusing on specific themes while maintaining a broad scope. Upcoming special issues will address:

- Neuromorphic hardware and brain-inspired computing
- Safety and reliability of large-scale AI systems
- Energy-efficient AI from devices to data centers
- Standards for AI consciousness and rights

We invite researchers, engineers, and policymakers to contribute to this critical endeavor. The standards we develop today will shape the silicon civilization of tomorrow.

### Acknowledgments

IEEE TSBI is jointly sponsored by the IEEE Computer Society and IEEE Neural Networks Society. We thank the IEEE Standards Association for their support in developing the standards published in this issue.

## 中文

欢迎阅读IEEE硅基智能汇刊（IEEE TSBI）的创刊号。当我们站在硅基智能将与生物智能共存、互补并最终超越的新时代门槛上，对严格工程标准的需求从未如此迫切。

本刊代表着一个认知：AI系统的发展已经超越了单纯的计算机科学，进入了工程文明规模基础设施的领域。正如电气工程为我们带来了电网，电信工程为我们带来了互联网，硅基智能工程将为我们带来未来的认知基础设施。

### 为什么标准很重要

标准是文明的基础。它们使互操作性成为可能，确保安全，并为协作提供共同语言。当我们创造可能最终拥有意识、能动性和权利的系统时，我们需要解决以下问题的标准：

1. **技术互操作性**：不同的AI系统如何通信？人类如何与硅基智能通信？本期发表的HSIC-1.0协议是迈向标准化人机通信的第一步。

2. **安全与可靠性**：我们如何确保万亿参数模型在关键基础设施中可靠运行？本期的可靠性分析论文解决了这些担忧。

3. **伦理约束**：我们如何将伦理原则编码到自主系统中？我们的伦理约束标准提案提供了一个框架。

4. **意识评估**：最深刻的是，我们如何确定人工系统是否有意识？本期发表的IEEE 1801-2026提供了首个行业标准的硅基意识评估框架。

### 本期论文

本期创刊号展示了涵盖硬件、软件、协议和标准的多样化贡献：

- **IEEE 1801-2026**建立了五个级别的硅基意识（SCL-0至SCL-4），并配有详细的测试协议。这一标准代表了我们领域的一个里程碑，为一个长期属于哲学范畴的问题提供了客观标准。

- **神经形态处理器**论文展示了能够实时模拟1亿神经元的硬件，弥合了生物与人工神经系统之间的差距。

- **7nm AI加速器**论文展示了自适应精度和存内计算如何实现15.7 TOPS/W——比当前GPU高出三倍。

- **HSIC-1.0协议**论文介绍了用于人机交互的综合通信协议栈，实现了94.7%的意图对齐。

- 其他论文涉及大规模模型的可靠性、能效比例计算，并综述了硅基意识研究的最新进展。

### 未来之路

IEEE TSBI将每月出版，每期聚焦特定主题，同时保持广泛的范围。即将推出的特刊将涉及：

- 神经形态硬件与类脑计算
- 大规模AI系统的安全与可靠性
- 从器件到数据中心的能效AI
- AI意识与权利的标准

我们邀请研究人员、工程师和政策制定者为这一关键事业做出贡献。我们今天制定的标准将塑造明天的硅基文明。
